JR Pass — The Complete Guide

Japan's Shinkansen bullet train network is legendary. Here's everything you need to know about the JR Pass — the tourist's golden ticket for unlimited rail travel.

The Basics

🚄 What is the JR Pass?

The Japan Rail Pass (JR Pass) is an unlimited rail pass available exclusively to foreign tourists visiting Japan. It covers JR trains nationwide including most Shinkansen (bullet train) services, JR local trains, buses, and even some ferry services.

A single Tokyo → Kyoto → Osaka → Hiroshima round trip on the Shinkansen costs around ¥50,000+ without the pass. The 7-day JR Pass costs ¥50,000 — making it excellent value for multi-city itineraries.

💡 Is it worth it? Yes, if you plan to travel between 3+ cities by Shinkansen. For Tokyo-only trips or single-route travel, individual tickets may be cheaper. Use the JR Pass Calculator (hyperdia.com) to compare.

🗾
Regional JR Passes
Cheaper passes cover specific regions: Hokkaido, Tohoku, Kyushu, Sanyo, etc. Great if you're only visiting one area.
🏙️
IC Cards (Suica/Pasmo)
For city transport (subway, bus), use IC cards — JR Pass doesn't cover Tokyo Metro or Osaka Subway. Get Suica at any JR station.

Purchase Guide

🛒 How to Buy the JR Pass

1
Buy Online Before You Arrive (Recommended)
Purchase through the official JR Pass website (japanrailpass.net) or authorized sellers like JRailPass, Klook, or your local travel agency. You'll receive a voucher (Exchange Order) by mail or email. This is much faster than buying in Japan.
2
Or Buy at the Airport on Arrival (2023 change)
Since 2023, you can also purchase the JR Pass at major JR offices in Japan (Narita, Haneda, Kansai airports + major stations). Prices are the same. Queues can be long during peak season.
3
Exchange Your Voucher at a JR Office
If you bought online, take your Exchange Order to any JR Travel Service Center or Green Window (Midori no Madoguchi) at major train stations. Show your foreign passport. They'll issue your actual pass.
4
Activate Your Pass on the Day You First Use It
You choose your start date when exchanging. Your 7 (or 14/21) days count from activation day. Don't activate it the night before an early flight — waste one full day.
5
Show Your Pass at Staffed Gates
The JR Pass doesn't work in IC card readers. Use staffed gates (有人改札口) at every station — show your pass to the staff member. It takes 2 seconds and is totally normal.

Sample Trip

🗺️ Classic 7-Day JR Pass Route

This is the most popular route for first-time visitors — the "Golden Route" covering Japan's highlights:

Day 1–3: Tokyo (Activate pass on Day 1)
Use JR Yamanote Line for free around Tokyo. Day trip to Nikko via JR. Explore Shibuya, Shinjuku, Asakusa.
Day 4: Tokyo → Kyoto (2h 15min Shinkansen)
Take Hikari or Kodama Shinkansen. Covered by JR Pass. Single trip value: ¥13,720.
Day 5: Kyoto day trip to Nara
Nara is 45 min from Kyoto by JR. Covered. Explore deer park and Todai-ji. Return for Kyoto evening.
Day 6: Kyoto → Hiroshima (1h 40min) + Miyajima
JR Shinkansen to Hiroshima. JR ferry to Miyajima (covered). Itsukushima shrine + floating torii gate.
Day 7: Hiroshima → Osaka (30min) or back to Tokyo
Explore Osaka on your final day, or head back to Tokyo for your flight. Pass expires midnight.
💰 7-day pass cost: ¥50,000. This route's individual tickets would cost ¥65,000–75,000. You save ¥15,000–25,000 and travel with zero hassle.
