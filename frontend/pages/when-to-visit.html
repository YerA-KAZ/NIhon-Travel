When to Visit Japan

Each season transforms Japan into something entirely different. Here's how to choose the perfect time for your journey.

Home› Travel Tips› When to Visit

Quick Answer

Best Time: March–May & October–November

Spring (cherry blossoms) and autumn (fall foliage) are universally considered the best times to visit Japan. Crowds are large but the scenery is unforgettable. Summer is hot and humid but offers festivals; winter is cold but crowd-free with beautiful snow scenery.

Pro tip: Book accommodation 3–6 months in advance for cherry blossom season (late March – early April). It's Japan's peak travel period and hotels fill up fast.

The Four Seasons

Season by Season

Spring
March – May

Cherry blossoms peak late March – early April in Tokyo and Kyoto.

Temperatures: 8–22°C. Ideal for sightseeing — warm but not humid.

Very busy, book months ahead.

Summer
June – August

Hot and humid (30–38°C). Rainy season in June–mid July.

Festivals (matsuri) everywhere — fireworks, Obon, Gion Festival.

Best time to climb Fuji (July–Aug).

Autumn
September – November

Koyo (fall foliage) peaks mid-November in Kyoto and Nikko.

Temperatures: 10–25°C. Comfortable and clear skies.

Second best season — slightly fewer crowds than spring.

Winter
December – February

Cold (0–10°C in Tokyo), snowy in Hokkaido and the Alps.

Ski resorts in Niseko and Hakuba are world-class.

Fewest tourists, best hotel deals.

Regional Guide

Japan's Climate Regions

Japan stretches 3,000km from north to south, so timing varies greatly by region.

北
Hokkaido (North)
Summers are refreshingly cool (20–25°C) — perfect escape from Tokyo heat. Winters bring heavy snow and world-class skiing in Niseko. Cherry blossoms arrive in late April–May.
東
Tokyo & Kanto Region
Four distinct seasons. Cherry blossoms peak around March 25 – April 10. Summers are hot (35°C+). Winters are dry and mild compared to most of Japan.
西
Kyoto, Osaka & Kansai
Hotter summers (38°C possible), colder winters with occasional snow. Spring and autumn are slightly earlier than Tokyo. November koyo here is legendary.
南
Okinawa (South)
Subtropical climate. Beach season April–October. Cherry blossoms in late January (earliest in Japan!). Avoid June–October for typhoon risk.

Special Events

Cherry Blossom Timing

The sakura front moves north from February (Okinawa) to May (Hokkaido). Here's the typical peak bloom timing:

Late January — Okinawa
Warm subtropical climate means Japan's earliest cherry blossoms, a different variety (Kanhizakura).
Mid March — Kyushu & Shikoku
Southern Japan opens the sakura season. Fukuoka and Hiroshima bloom around March 20–25.
Late March – Early April — Tokyo & Kyoto
Peak bloom in the most popular destinations. Usually March 28 – April 5 in Tokyo; a few days later in Kyoto.
Mid April — Tohoku Region
Hirosaki Castle Park in Aomori is one of Japan's most spectacular hanami sites, peak around April 20–30.
Late April – May — Hokkaido
Sapporo and the northern island are last to bloom, with peak usually late April to early May.
Cherry blossom dates shift by 1–2 weeks each year depending on winter temperatures. Check Japan Meteorological Corporation's official forecast (sakura.weathermap.jp) the winter before your trip.
