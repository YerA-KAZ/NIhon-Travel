At a Glance

12 Quick Tips for Every Traveller

The most important things to know, condensed.

Carry Cash
Many restaurants, shrines and small shops are cash-only. 7-Eleven ATMs reliably accept foreign cards.
Never Tip
Tipping is not part of Japanese culture and can be seen as rude or confusing. The price includes everything.
Get a SIM or WiFi
Rent a pocket WiFi or buy a data SIM at the airport. Essential for Google Maps and Google Translate.
Learn 5 Japanese Words
Arigatō, sumimasen, onegaishimasu, itadakimasu, and kanpai. Locals will light up when you use them.
Carry a Trash Bag
Public bins are extremely rare in Japan. Carry a small bag for wrappers and bottles until you find a convenience store bin.
Quiet on Trains
Phone calls are strictly taboo on public transport. Keep voice low. Use headphones. Priority seats are for elderly and pregnant.
Slip-On Shoes
You'll remove shoes constantly — at ryokan, many restaurants, temples. Wear shoes you can take off quickly and easily.
Love Your Convenience Store
7-Eleven, Lawson and FamilyMart sell genuinely delicious food, hot drinks, medicines, ATM access and more. They're lifesavers.
Walk on the Left
Escalators: stand on the left (except in Osaka — stand on the right). Walk on the left side of paths and corridors.
Download Offline Maps
Download your Japan cities offline in Google Maps before arriving. Also download Google Translate with Japanese for camera translation.
Layer Your Clothes
Japan's seasons are extreme. Check forecasts daily. Summers are brutal (35°C+) — lightweight, breathable fabrics are essential.
Get Travel Insurance
Japan's healthcare is excellent but expensive for foreigners without insurance. Typhoons and natural events can also disrupt travel plans.
