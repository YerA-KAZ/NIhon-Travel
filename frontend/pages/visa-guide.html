Japan Visa Guide

Everything you need to know about entering Japan — from visa-free access to application steps, documents and arrival tips.

Good News First

🎉 Visa-Free Access for Many Countries

Japan grants visa-free entry (visa waiver) to citizens of over 70 countries for stays up to 90 days for tourism. No prior application or visit to an embassy is needed — you simply arrive, pass immigration, and you're in.

✅ Visa-free countries include: USA, UK, EU countries, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, South Korea, Singapore, most Western nations. Your passport is your visa.
⚠️ Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, and most Central Asian countries require a tourist visa. Apply at your nearest Japanese embassy 2–4 weeks before travel.

For Countries Requiring a Visa

📋 How to Apply — Step by Step

1
Find Your Nearest Japanese Embassy or Consulate
Japan has embassies in Astana (Kazakhstan), Tashkent (Uzbekistan), Baku (Azerbaijan) and other major cities. Check the official Ministry of Foreign Affairs Japan website for the correct office.
2
Prepare Required Documents
Gather all required documents (see checklist below). Incomplete applications are returned immediately. Have both originals and copies ready.
3
Submit at the Embassy
Submit in person or via a registered travel agency. Most Japanese embassies do not accept postal applications for first-time applicants.
4
Wait for Processing (5–10 Business Days)
Standard processing takes 5 working days. Apply at least 3–4 weeks before your planned travel date. Express processing is not typically available.
5
Collect Your Passport with Visa
Pick up in person or arrange collection through your travel agency. Check the visa dates carefully — single-entry visas cannot be extended inside Japan.

Document Checklist

📁 Required Documents

📘
Valid Passport
At least 6 months validity beyond your return date. At least 2 blank pages. Original + copy of main page.
📸
Visa Application Photo
45mm × 45mm, white background, taken within 6 months. No glasses, neutral expression. Printed (not digital).
📝
Application Form
Downloaded from the embassy website. Filled in black ink (or typed). Signed by applicant. No corrections with correction fluid.
✈️
Flight Itinerary
Confirmed round-trip flight booking. A booking confirmation (not a ticket) is acceptable — you don't need to purchase yet.
🏨
Hotel Reservations
Booking confirmation for all nights in Japan. Use cancellable bookings on Booking.com or Hotels.com during the application process.
💰
Bank Statement
Last 3–6 months of bank statements. Must show sufficient funds (approx. $100/day or equivalent). Official stamp from the bank required.
💼
Employment Proof
Employment certificate or letter from employer confirming your position, salary and approved leave. Self-employed: business registration documents.
📅
Travel Itinerary
Day-by-day plan of your visit: cities you'll visit, activities planned. Can be self-prepared — no official format required.
ℹ️ Requirements can change. Always verify the current checklist on your country's Japanese Embassy website before applying. Some embassies may request additional documents.

Arriving in Japan

✈️ What Happens at the Airport

①
Immigration (入国審査)
Join the "Non-Japanese Passport" queue. You will be fingerprinted (both index fingers) and photographed. Have your return ticket and hotel address ready. Officers may ask about your plans.
②
Customs Declaration Card
Fill in the paper card on the plane or use the electronic kiosk at arrival. Declare any goods exceeding your duty-free allowance. Customs in Japan is strict — declare honestly.
③
Baggage Claim & Customs
Collect your bags, then pass through customs. Green lane (nothing to declare) or red lane (items to declare). Spot checks are common. Do not bring: drugs of any kind, certain medications, more than ¥1,000,000 cash undeclared.
④
Get Your IC Card & SIM
Before leaving the airport: pick up your pocket WiFi or SIM card (pre-ordered online), and get a Suica or Pasmo IC card from any station machine. Both are essential for smooth travel.
🚫 Important: Many common medications are prohibited in Japan, including codeine-containing drugs, some ADHD medications (Adderall), and certain stimulants. Check the Ministry of Health's import regulations before packing any prescription drugs.
